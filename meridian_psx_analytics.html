Methodology & definitions

Annualised return
Compound annual growth rate — the steady yearly rate that turns the start value into the end value over the period (252 trading days/year).
Volatility
Standard deviation of daily returns, annualised (×√252). A measure of how much the value swings.
Max drawdown
The largest peak-to-trough decline over the period — the worst loss an investor holding throughout would have endured.
Sharpe ratio
(Annualised return − risk-free rate) ÷ volatility. Return earned per unit of risk taken. Risk-free rate is editable above because Pakistan's rates move a lot.
Beta
Sensitivity to the KSE-100. Beta 1.2 means the holding tends to move 1.2× the market.
Portfolio
Weighted blend of selected holdings, rebalanced to the weights shown. Equal weight if left at default.
